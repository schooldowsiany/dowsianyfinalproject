High Protein Buffalo Chicken Dip/Burritos

Servings: 7

Protein: ~25–30g per serving

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs (900g) boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1 cup plain Greek yogurt (non-fat or 2%)
  • ½ cup low-fat cream cheese, softened
  • ½ cup buffalo sauce (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp onion powder
  • ½ tsp paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: ¼ cup reduced-fat shredded cheddar cheese (for topping)
  • Optional garnish: chopped green onions

Instructions:

  1. Place chicken breasts in the crock pot.
  2. In a bowl, mix Greek yogurt, cream cheese, buffalo sauce, garlic powder, onion powder, paprika, salt, and pepper.
  3. Pour the sauce over the chicken in the crock pot.
  4. Cook on low for 6 hours or high for 3–4 hours until chicken is fully cooked and tender.
  5. Remove chicken and shred with two forks, then return to the crock pot and mix with the sauce.
  6. Optional: Sprinkle cheddar cheese on top during the last 10 minutes to melt.
  7. Serve as a dip or use as a filling for burritos, wraps, or over a salad.
  8. Divide into 7 containers for easy grab-and-go meals.
  9. Store in the fridge for up to 5 days or freeze for up to 3 months.

Crock Pot Healthy BBQ Chicken Burrito

Servings: 7

Protein: ~30–35g per burrito

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs (900g) boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1 cup low-sugar BBQ sauce
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup corn (fresh, frozen, or canned, drained)
  • 1 cup diced bell peppers (any color)
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • ½ tsp black pepper
  • ½ tsp chili powder (optional)
  • 7 whole wheat tortillas (large, burrito-sized)
  • Optional toppings: shredded cheese, Greek yogurt, avocado, cilantro

Instructions:

  1. Place chicken breasts at the bottom of the crock pot.
  2. Add black beans, corn, bell peppers, onion, and spices on top of the chicken.
  3. Pour BBQ sauce over all the ingredients.
  4. Cook on low for 6–7 hours or high for 3–4 hours until chicken is fully cooked and tender.
  5. Remove chicken and shred with two forks, then mix it back into the crock pot with the sauce and vegetables.
  6. To assemble burritos: spoon ~1 portion of the mixture into a tortilla, add optional toppings, and wrap tightly.
  7. Assemble burritos individually and wrap in foil or parchment paper for easy grab-and-go meals.
  8. Store in the fridge for up to 5 days or freeze for up to 3 months.
  9. If reheating from frozen, microwave for 2–3 minutes or bake at 350°F (175°C) for 15–20 minutes until heated through.

Mexican Beef Burrito

Servings: 7

Protein: ~30–35g per burrito

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs (900g) lean ground beef or beef stew meat
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup corn (fresh, frozen, or canned, drained)
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes with green chilies (or regular diced tomatoes)
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • ½ tsp smoked paprika
  • ½ tsp oregano
  • ½ tsp black pepper
  • Salt to taste
  • 7 whole wheat tortillas (large, burrito-sized)
  • Optional toppings: shredded cheese, Greek yogurt, avocado, cilantro, salsa

Instructions:

  1. Place beef in the crock pot. If using stew meat, no pre-cooking is needed; if using ground beef, brown it lightly in a skillet first.
  2. Add black beans, corn, diced tomatoes, onion, bell pepper, garlic, and spices. Stir to combine.
  3. Cook on low for 6–7 hours or high for 3–4 hours until beef is tender and flavors are blended.
  4. If using stew meat, shred it with forks; if using ground beef, stir well to break it up.
  5. To assemble burritos: spoon ~1 portion of the mixture into a tortilla, add optional toppings, and wrap tightly.
